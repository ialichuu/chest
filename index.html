About Me

I studied at The University of Texas at Dallas majoring with a B.S. in Cognitive Science and minoring in Business Administration. I am currently pursuing a career in User Experience Design and User Experience Research.

My interest in technology and design began when I was a child, inspired by video games and anime. However, I was also engrossed in the medical field throughout high school and some of college, becoming a certified nursing assistant (CNA), taking healthcare-related courses, and participating in HOSA – Future Health Professionals events. I also incorporated my interest in art through HOSA competitions by participating in the Extemporaneous Health Poster competition in high school, getting third place in regionals, and making it to State.

While I was working to pursue the medical field, I participated in art contests like Visual Arts Scholastic Event (VASE), took some drawing and ceramic-making classes, but art was never my main focus. I also had a strong interest in psychology — listening to people and wondering why they acted the way they did.

After taking upa business minor in college, working at a digital marketing company, and creating graphics for my friend’s startup company, I started to wonder if I could incorporate my love for drawing and psychology into a career. Thus, I rethought my path and changed into Cognitive Science which was the perfect major for my interests, incorporating Human-Computer Interactions and Psychology.

As we move more toward a technology-focused society, it’s crucial we make it easy for all sorts of people to be able to use the apps and products made by innovators. For me, UX is the perfect mix of design and technology. It envelops both my passion and hunger for creating products that everyone can enjoy and to make a meaningful yet positive impact in this world.

My other hobbies include playing games, such as Minecraft, Overwatch, and League of Legends on my PC, listening to scientific and technological podcasts, such as Today Explained and Reset, working out, and drawing.
