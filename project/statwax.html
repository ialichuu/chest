Statwax

Designing the logo for the Dallas regional headquarters

Overview

Statwax is a performance marketing agency that delivers data-based, scalable campaigns for B2B, higher education, and SaaS companies that produce real results. We leverage a deep understanding of potential buyer behaviors and years of industry-specific acquisition data to fundamentally change the way lead generation-focused companies approach prospects and the rapidly evolving digital behaviors they exhibit.

Headquartered in Fishers, IN, Statwax has expanded to Detroit, MI and Dallas, TX. During my internship at Statwax, I created a regional design of the company logo to represent and establish an identity for the Dallas branch.

The Challenge

How can I make the regional design of the Dallas logo look unique yet incorporate the original logo?

My Role

This was a design challenge provided by Statwax. I am currently affiliated with the company.

Methods/Tools Used

Prototyping | Adobe Photoshop | Adobe Illustrator

My Process

Initial Design

I created several versions of the design, leaving the logo itself untouched. The first few designs were simple additions that included “Dallas” as part of the name. I chose sans serif fonts to match the original design of the logo. In addition, I kept the colors of each design a different shade of blue to keep the identity of Statwax.

❮ ❯

I surveyed a group of 10 individuals what came to mind when they thought of the city Dallas. Six out of ten said the Dallas skyline or the Reunion Tower. Therefore, I added downtown Dallas’ city skyline as the background for the next part of the design. I experimented with the color and placement of the skyline.

❮ ❯

Final Design

Takeaways

Overall, this project was a pleasant learning experience for me. I was able to work alongside the company and get opinions from the employees as well as people outside of the company. If I were to do this project again, I would have done the designs in Adobe Illustrator instead of Adobe Photoshop to make it vectorized. In addition, I could have focused on solidifying the design first, then going in with the colors.
